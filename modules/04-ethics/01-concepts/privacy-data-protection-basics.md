# Privacy and Data Protection Basics

## What it is
Practices to minimize data exposure, comply with policies, and protect users.

## Why it matters
Privacy failures create legal, reputational, and user harm risks.

## Core concepts
- Data minimization and purpose limitation
- Consent, notice, and user control
- PII handling and de-identification

## Common failure modes
- Collecting more data than needed
- Re-identification through joins or leakage
- Inadequate access controls

## Engineering checklist
- Inventory data sources and retention
- Apply least-privilege access
- Document consent and user-facing disclosures

## References
- GDPR and CCPA summaries (non-legal)
- ISO/IEC 27701 Privacy Information Management
