# Probability and Distributions Basics

## What it is
Foundational rules for uncertainty (probability axioms) and common
distributions used to model events, counts, and noise.

## Why it matters
Many system decisions are probabilistic: reliability, risk, and A/B test
outcomes rely on correct distributional assumptions.

## Core ideas
- Conditional probability, Bayes rule, independence
- Distributions: Bernoulli, Binomial, Poisson, Normal
- Expectation and variance

## Example
Error rates in a service can be modeled as Bernoulli trials; the number of
errors in a window often approximates a Poisson process.

## Pitfalls
- Treating dependent events as independent
- Overusing the Normal distribution for skewed data
- Ignoring base rates in Bayes reasoning
