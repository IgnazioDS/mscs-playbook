# Linear Algebra: Vectors and Matrices

## What it is
A minimal linear algebra toolkit: vectors, matrices, basic operations, and
geometric interpretations.

## Why it matters
Linear algebra underpins ML models, optimization, and numerical computation.
You need it for feature scaling, projections, and matrix-based algorithms.

## Core ideas
- Vectors: dot product, norm, cosine similarity
- Matrices: multiplication, transpose, determinants
- Systems of equations and linear transforms

## Example
Cosine similarity between two embedding vectors is a dot product normalized by
vector norms.

## Pitfalls
- Mixing row/column conventions
- Silent shape mismatches in matrix multiplication
- Using ill-conditioned matrices without regularization
