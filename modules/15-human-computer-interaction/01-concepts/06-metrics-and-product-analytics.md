# Metrics and Product Analytics

## Overview
Metrics translate user behavior into measurable signals that help prioritize
changes and evaluate impact.

## Key Concepts
- Leading vs lagging indicators.
- Funnel and cohort analysis.
- Retention, activation, and conversion.
- Guardrail metrics to prevent regressions.

## Practical Notes
- Tie each metric to a product decision.
- Define events with clear naming and properties.
- Use baselines and targets to interpret movement.

## Common Pitfalls
- Vanity metrics without decision value.
- Event schema drift over time.
- Optimizing metrics that misalign with user goals.

## Checklist
- Metrics mapped to business and user outcomes.
- Tracking plan reviewed and instrumented.
- Dashboards include context and guardrails.
